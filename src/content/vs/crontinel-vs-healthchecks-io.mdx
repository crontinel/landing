Healthchecks.io is a focused, well-built tool. It does one thing well: if your cron job doesn't ping within the expected window, it sends you an alert. That's valuable. For many teams with simple scheduled tasks, it's exactly what they need.

For Laravel apps running Horizon, it's not enough.

## What Healthchecks.io does

Healthchecks.io works on the dead-man switch model. You register a check, get a unique ping URL, and add a curl call to your cron job:

```bash
* * * * * cd /var/www && php artisan send:invoices && curl -fsS --retry 3 https://hc-ping.com/your-uuid > /dev/null
```

If the ping doesn't arrive in the expected window, Healthchecks.io sends an alert. You can configure integrations: email, Slack, PagerDuty, webhooks, and more.

This model is reliable for the scenarios it covers.

## The gap: what happens between pings

Healthchecks.io has no visibility into anything happening inside your application between pings. It can tell you a job ran (or didn't). It cannot tell you:

- Whether Horizon has workers running
- Whether a supervisor is paused
- How many jobs are queued in each queue
- How long the oldest queued job has been waiting
- How many jobs are failing per minute

These are exactly the signals that matter when your application is degrading silently. A supervisor can pause, jobs can pile up to thousands, and downstream services can start timing out, all while Healthchecks.io reports green because the cron ping arrived on schedule.

## The instrumentation requirement

Every cron job that Healthchecks.io monitors needs a curl ping added to it. That means:

- Instrumenting every scheduled command individually
- Remembering to add pings when you add new commands
- Handling ping failures (the `--retry 3` in the curl call)
- Maintaining the mapping between jobs and their ping URLs

Crontinel hooks into Laravel's `ScheduledTaskFinished` and `ScheduledTaskFailed` events automatically. Once the package is installed, all scheduled tasks are recorded with no per-task instrumentation.

## Pricing

Healthchecks.io has a generous free tier (20 checks, 100 pings/check/month). Paid plans start at $20/month for more checks and team features.

Crontinel is free for one app with 5 monitors and 7 days of history. Pro is $19/month for up to 5 apps with 90 days of history. Team is $49/month with unlimited apps and team access. The OSS package is MIT-licensed and works without the SaaS backend.

## When Healthchecks.io is the right choice

Healthchecks.io is a solid pick when:
- You run simple cron jobs outside of Laravel
- Dead-man switch monitoring is your primary need
- You want to self-host (they publish their source)
- You need a very generous free tier for many small checks

## When Crontinel fits better

Choose Crontinel when:
- Your application uses Laravel Horizon
- You need visibility into queue depth and supervisor state
- You want monitoring that works without modifying every job
- Your background jobs are revenue-critical and need fast alerting

## Installing Crontinel

```bash
composer require crontinel/laravel
php artisan crontinel:install
```

Add your alert channel to `.env`:

```bash
CRONTINEL_ALERT_CHANNEL=slack
CRONTINEL_SLACK_WEBHOOK=https://hooks.slack.com/services/...
```

That's it. Every scheduled task starts recording, and Horizon monitoring starts immediately if Horizon is running.
